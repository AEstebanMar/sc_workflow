<details>
<br>

Metrics from the FASTQ files:

- <strong>Number of sequences</strong>: Total number of sequences in the FASTQ file. More sequences means more sequencing depth.
- <strong>Read maximum length</strong>: Maximum read length. It is usual that R2 reads have more length than R1.
- <strong>Read minimum length</strong>: Minimum read legnth. They do not have to be the same as the maximum reading lengths, but they should be consistent with them.
- <strong>GC content</strong>: Guanine-cytosine content. Percentage of nitrogenous bases that are either guanine (G) or cytosine (C). We expect values around 50%.
- <strong>Quality scores - mean quality per base</strong>: Quality is measured with the Phred score (Q), which is typically in the range 2 - 40, but higher and lower values are sometimes used. The higher the Phred score, the higher the quality.
- <strong>Quality scores - mean quality per base in lower quartile</strong>: Same as the previous one, but focusing in the lower quartile.
- <strong>Quality scores - mean quality per base in 10th decile</strong>: Same as the previous one, but focusing in the tenth decile.
- <strong>Quality scores - weigthed quality per sequence</strong>: Same as the previous one, but the mean is calculated by sequence and weighted by sequence length.
- <strong>Mean indeterminations per base</strong>: Mean of the percent of bases at each position with no base call (N).
- <strong>Weigthed read length</strong>: Weighted mean of read length.

</details>
